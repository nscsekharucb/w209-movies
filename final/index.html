Behind the Scenes

Visualizing the data of the film industry

Surya Nimmagadda
Maura Cullen
Alex Lau

W209-2 Fall 2017

Our project uses D3 to visualize various aspects of American film data collected from 2008 through 2013. Our focus was primarily on the relationships between genres, review scores, domestic gross revenue (opening week and overall), profitability, and release windows. These exploratory visualizations are intended to highlight interesting relationships and serve as tools for discovering further insights. The primary audience includes film fans and those interested in testing their own preconceptions of the business side of the movie industry. Are big blockbusters also big money makers? Is it wise to release a movie in the Spring if one wants to maximize profits? Welcome to our behind the scenes look at movie metrics!

Due to differences between critic and audience scores, we created a blended score by weighting both equally. In the tooltip for a film, the critic-to-audience ratio shows the relationship between these scores. A value of 1.0 means that both critics and audiences reviews were in agreement. A value greater than 1.0 results from critics reviewing the film higher than audiences, while the opposite is true for films with a ratio less than 1.0.
